Who Am I?

I’ve always had trouble whenever asked to write a paragraph about myself. My first reaction is to think about my current interests, but sometimes the current interests don’t feel right. Like okay, I just watched 3 seasons of Schitt’s Creek. Or I just played the video game that my friends really like for the past 2 months. However, neither of those seem fair to represent me as a person. As I’ve recently given it more thought, I realized the parts that make me… me are the pieces that you’d learn by knowing my past. So let’s start.

Family Stuff

I am an only child (kind of). I do have a half-sister who lived with us until she went to the military, but she is 10 years older than me. As a result we never really had a sisterly bond, she often tried to act as another parent for me. That usually rubbed me the wrong way. It also meant that she left before I even went to middle school, making me more or less an only child. I still love her, but I don’t know if we will ever have the sibling bond I’ve always craved. My parents are from Minnesota / North Dakota. They are both engineers, but my mom has always been more of my role model. Everything I do in school or life, I hope to make her proud. My dad’s influence can be seen more in the other topics that will come up. I also have a dog, a mini schnauzer named Sally.

The Candy Store

Something I frequently forget as being a part of my unique experience is the candy store. During my early formative years (elementary & middle school), my dad owned a candy store called Sweet Street. On the off chance you remember it, it was in the Twin Peaks mall first and then it moved to the Southwest Plaza when they began threatening to turn Twin Peaks into an outdoor shopping mall. I spent many weekends there, organizing the stuffed animals, sanitizing the dishes, checking the shelves for empties, providing input on merchandise, and playing Webkinz on the computer. As a reward for my hard work, I would frequently get stuffed animals. I admit I have quite the collection. I also got to taste test a lot of the candies, both new ones and old classics. It was a magical place and I’m still bitter that we had to sell it because the shopping mall was getting less popular. If only we could’ve gotten into the Flatirons mall… Sweet Street was significantly better than Fuzziwigs. The things I miss the most are the fresh fudge we made and the Reese’s Whipps bars (which they stopped selling!!). It was the childhood many would probably dream of.

Video Games

As a candy store owner, my dad was always a little more fun inclined. Though I suppose the cause is probably not actually the candy store, it is just his spirit. He was a little bit of a gamer and I always remember him playing on the original XBOX. Before I was allowed to actually play any of the games, I was permitted to watch when he played Halo or Hunter: The Reckoning. Probably both a little mature for my age, but it didn’t really affect me. After a little while I convinced him to let me play. Halo was probably my first video game, and I played the campaigns up to number 4 with my dad. I should also mention Super Monkey Ball was an early classic I played. We also had the original playstation and classics such as Crash Bandicoot, Spyro, and Poy Poy. The Wii was also one of our family favorites. As I grew more interested in games I began to play more on my own. Toontown, Wizard101, Assassin’s Creed, and Dragon Age being the most notable examples. To this day I remain a fan of some of the older games and I find myself wishing I had more time to play.

Future Aspirations

Every kid dreams of being something. When I was really young I was not camera shy in any ways. I was a performer, ready to show the audience what they wanted. I have no idea where that dream went, because most of what I remember is me being a shy and awkward individual. That includes right now. However, perhaps a little bit of that passion stayed hidden. Today I wish I could become a fairly popular creator of some kind: YouTuber, streamer, or maybe a podcaster. We’ll see if I ever truly put the effort into it. If you had asked my younger self what I was going to be when I grow up I had a very clear path. I was going to be a Veterinarian, because I loved animals. And my backup option, if I ever needed it, was to be a model. I suppose there’s a lot of irony that my middle school and high school years were spent in strong opposition to the existence of makeup. Clearly I wasn’t very committed to the effort required in being a model.

Defining Interests

I had two major defining themes of my childhood: pirates and big cats. The interest in pirates was caused by watching the Pirates of the Carribean movies. They have always been one of my absolute favorite movies. Big cats just came from the interest of loving animals as a child. Some kids get really into dinosaurs or horses, I got really into tigers, lions, etc. Unfortunately, I eventually learned that I am allergic to cats. Despite living with one for years, we never noticed until we visited a family friend who actually had friendly cats. That was a weird day. I still love those big pawed predators though.

Who I am Today

I read webcomics, but once I start one I have to read it constantly. I get invested and it’s so easy to just spend an hour reading. It drains my phone battery and I am practically impossible to pull away from it, even if someone is trying to talk to me. I play Beat Saber on my dad’s Oculus Quest and it has quickly become one of my favorite games. Two nights ago I spent two hours playing and I could’ve kept going. Online I game with my friends, we usually play Monster Hunter World, League of Legends (because it’s there), and various random games. I focus on school work during the day, because I am still a college student, but I get a lot less stressed about it than I used to. As a TAM student I enjoy all of my classes and sometimes I get carried away thinking of extremely narrow career goals based on specific subjects. However, web development actually interests me the most because it is constantly changing. I like knowing that it is a field I will never stop learning in. Well that’s about it. That is who I am. This is who Cassidy is.
